Size Sorts and p-Hacking

Jun He May 31, 2026

Sorting on firm size recovers the classic size effect — but the chapter's deeper lesson is methodological: how many defensible choices stand between raw data and a published result.

The size effect

Sorting stocks by market capitalization and forming a small-minus-big spread historically produced a positive average return. It is one of the oldest documented anomalies, and a natural first sort because size is cleanly measured.

Researcher degrees of freedom

The same hypothesis can be tested many ways: which exchange's breakpoints to use, whether to weight equally or by value, how to filter penny stocks or financials, which sample period to study. Each choice is defensible, yet together they form a garden of forking paths. Trying combinations until one is significant is p-hacking, even when no single step feels dishonest.

Guarding against it

The defenses are transparency and robustness: report results across reasonable specifications rather than the most flattering one, decide the design before seeing the outcome, and treat a finding that survives only one particular combination of choices with suspicion. A real effect should be visible under many sensible setups.
