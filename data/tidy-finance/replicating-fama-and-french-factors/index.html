Replicating Fama–French Factors

Jun He May 31, 2026

Building the SMB and HML factors from scratch is both a useful skill and a test of whether you can reproduce a canonical result — a microcosm of the replication challenges in empirical finance.

The construction

The Fama–French recipe combines a double sort on size and book-to-market. Stocks split into two size groups and three value groups, giving six portfolios. SMB (small minus big) averages the small portfolios minus the big ones; HML (high minus low) averages the high book-to-market portfolios minus the low ones. The construction deliberately neutralizes each factor with respect to the other.

Details that matter

Faithful replication hinges on specifics: NYSE breakpoints (not all-stock breakpoints), the June rebalancing convention, the correct accounting lag so book equity is known when portfolios form, and consistent value weighting. Each is a place where a reasonable-looking shortcut shifts the factor.

Checking the replication

The test is to correlate your home-grown factors with the published series. A high correlation confirms the pipeline is sound; a gap points back to one of the construction details. Getting close — but rarely identical — is itself an honest lesson about how sensitive these benchmarks are to implementation.
