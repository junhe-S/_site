Cleaning Enhanced TRACE

Jun He May 31, 2026

The Enhanced TRACE feed of corporate-bond transactions is not analysis-ready as delivered. A careful cleaning routine is needed to turn the raw message log into a series of genuine, executed trades.

Why cleaning is needed

The raw data is a record of messages, not final trades. It includes reports that were later cancelled, corrected, or reversed, as well as duplicates from the two-sided reporting of inter-dealer trades. Taking the feed at face value double-counts volume and contaminates prices.

The steps

Cleaning proceeds by matching and removing the messages that undo or amend earlier ones: drop trades flagged as cancellations and the originals they cancel, apply corrections, remove reversals, and collapse the duplicate dealer reports of a single trade into one record. Filters on price and size remove obvious errors.

The result

What remains is a clean panel of executed corporate-bond trades — price, size, time, and side — suitable for computing yields, spreads, and liquidity measures. Because the procedure is intricate and easy to get subtly wrong, it is documented as a standalone routine that other analyses can reuse.
