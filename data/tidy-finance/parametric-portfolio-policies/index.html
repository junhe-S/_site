Parametric Portfolio Policies

Jun He May 31, 2026

Parametric portfolio policies sidestep the hardest part of mean–variance optimization — estimating expected returns and a huge covariance matrix — by modelling portfolio weights directly as a function of firm characteristics.

The idea

Instead of estimating inputs and then optimizing, start from a benchmark (say, value weights) and tilt each stock's weight up or down according to its characteristics — size, value, momentum. The tilt is linear in the characteristics, governed by a small number of coefficients.

Estimating the tilts

The coefficients are chosen to maximize the investor's expected utility of the resulting portfolio return, estimated directly over the sample. Because only a handful of parameters are estimated — one per characteristic, not one per stock — the approach scales to large universes and avoids the instability of estimating thousands of moments.

Why it appeals

The method, due to Brandt, Santa-Clara, and Valkanov, is parsimonious and interpretable: a positive coefficient on a characteristic says "tilt toward stocks with more of it." It connects the asset-pricing evidence on characteristics directly to an implementable portfolio, without ever forming an explicit covariance matrix.
