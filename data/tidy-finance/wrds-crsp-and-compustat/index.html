WRDS, CRSP, and Compustat

Jun He May 31, 2026

CRSP and Compustat, accessed through WRDS, are the backbone datasets of empirical equity research. Building a clean, merged panel from them is a rite of passage — and a place where small mistakes propagate into every later result.

CRSP

CRSP provides stock-market data: prices, returns, shares outstanding, and trading volume, along with identifiers and exchange/delisting information. Two details matter a great deal — adjusting returns for delisting (ignoring them biases results upward) and computing market capitalization consistently as price times shares outstanding.

Compustat

Compustat provides accounting fundamentals from firm financial statements. The key timing concern is that accounting data is only known after a reporting lag, so you must lag fundamentals appropriately before matching them to returns — otherwise you use information that was not yet public, a classic look-ahead bias.

Merging into a panel

The two databases use different identifiers, so a linking table bridges CRSP's permno and Compustat's gvkey. The result is a firm-month panel of returns, market cap, and characteristics — the dataset that the asset-pricing chapters sort, regress, and form portfolios on.
