Fixed Effects and Clustered Standard Errors

Jun He May 31, 2026

Panel data — many firms observed over many periods — needs regression tools that respect its structure. Fixed effects control for unobserved heterogeneity; clustered standard errors get the inference right.

Fixed effects

A firm fixed effect absorbs everything about a firm that is constant over time (its industry, its business model), so the coefficient of interest is identified from variation within the firm. A time fixed effect absorbs whatever is common to all firms in a period (the macro environment). Including both — two-way fixed effects — strips out both unchanging firm traits and common shocks, leaving the idiosyncratic variation that usually carries the causal content.

Clustered standard errors

Residuals in a firm panel are correlated: across time within a firm (persistence) and across firms within a period (common shocks). Treating observations as independent badly understates standard errors. Clustering — by firm, by time, or both (two-way) — allows for these correlations and produces honest confidence intervals.

In practice

The two tools are complementary: fixed effects address bias from omitted, fixed confounders; clustering addresses inference given dependence. Most modern panel regressions in finance report both, and the choice of clustering dimension follows the suspected correlation structure of the residuals.
