Factor Selection via Machine Learning

Jun He May 31, 2026

Hundreds of characteristics have been proposed as return predictors — the "factor zoo." Machine-learning methods offer a disciplined way to choose among many correlated candidates rather than testing them one at a time.

The high-dimensional problem

With many predictors, ordinary regression overfits: it fits noise, and coefficients become unstable when predictors are correlated. The task is to select a parsimonious set that predicts out of sample, not just in sample.

Penalized regression

The lasso adds a penalty on the absolute size of coefficients, which shrinks many to exactly zero — performing variable selection automatically. Ridge shrinks coefficients toward zero without eliminating them, taming collinearity; the elastic net blends the two. The penalty strength is a tuning parameter chosen by cross-validation.

Honest evaluation

The discipline that makes this work is out-of-sample validation: tune on a training period, evaluate on data the model never saw, and respect the time ordering so the future never leaks into the past. Selecting factors this way confronts the zoo directly — keeping the predictors that earn their place and discarding the rest.
