Accessing and Managing Financial Data

Jun He May 31, 2026

Once a project uses more than one dataset, how you store and retrieve data becomes as important as the analysis. A little discipline here keeps later chapters fast and reproducible.

Open-source datasets

Many of the series an empirical project needs are free: the Fama–French factors and the risk-free rate, the q-factors, momentum, and a range of macro series. Scripting these downloads — rather than clicking through a website — means the data pipeline is documented and repeatable.

A local database

Rather than re-downloading or juggling CSV files, store cleaned data in a local database (SQLite is enough for most work). The database becomes the single source of truth: each script reads exactly the table it needs and writes results back, and large panels load far faster than from flat files.

Tidy storage

Keep tables in tidy form — one row per observation, consistent keys (identifier and date) — so that joining returns to characteristics to factors is a routine operation. The upfront cost of a clean schema is repaid every time you run an analysis on top of it.
