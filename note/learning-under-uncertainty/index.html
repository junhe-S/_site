Note

This chapter studies asset pricing when agents learn about an unknown parameter. The log aggregate dividend \(\delta_t=\ln D_t\) has unknown drift \(\mu_\delta\), taking one of the discrete values \(\{\mu_1,\dots,\mu_n\}\). В§16.1 uses Bayes' rule to derive the continuous-time updating equation for the belief \(\Pi_t(\mu_s)\), finding that the belief innovation \(d\hat Z_t\) is still a standard Brownian motion and the belief process is a martingale; under homogeneous agents it characterizes the price-dividend ratio and risk premium, and shows learning lowers the risk premium iff the subjective-weighted drift is below the objective conditional mean; it then extends to heterogeneous agents and a regime-shifting model. В§16.2 gives continuous-time Kalman filtering: an observation equation + a state equation, deriving the latent state \(\beta_t\)'s mean dynamics (16.37) and (Riccati-type) variance dynamics (16.39) via joint normality and a conditional-normal lemma (Lemma 16.1).

16.1 Homogeneous Beliefs and Discrete Set of States

16.1.1 Setup

еҗҢиҙЁд»ЈзҗҶдәәпјҲеҗҢдҝЎеҝөпјҢеҸҜи§ҶдёәеҚ•дёҖд»ЈзҗҶдәәпјүгҖӮдёҖеҸӘиӮЎзҘЁд»· \(P_t\)гҖҒжҙҫжҒҜ \(D_t\)пјҢ\(\delta_t\equiv\ln D_t\) ж»Ўи¶і (16.1)пјҡ

Homogeneous agents (same beliefs, treated as one agent). A single stock with price \(P_t\) pays \(D_t\), with \(\delta_t\equiv\ln D_t\) satisfying (16.1):

$$d\delta_t=\mu_\delta\,dt+\sigma_\delta\,dZ_t,\tag{16.1}$$

\(\{Z_t\}\) ж ҮеҮҶеёғжң—иҝҗеҠЁпјҢ\(\sigma_\delta\) е·ІзҹҘпјҢ\(\mu_\delta\) жңӘзҹҘдё”еҸӘеҸ– \(\{\mu_1,\dots,\mu_n\}\) дёӯжҹҗдёҖеҖјгҖҒж°ёдёҚеҸҳеҠЁгҖӮ\(\{\mathcal F_t\}\) дёәдҝЎжҒҜжөҒгҖӮд»ЈзҗҶдәәзҡ„дё»и§Ӯе…ҲйӘҢпјҲ0 жңҹпјүдҝЎеҝө \(\Pi_0(\mu_s)=\mathbf P\{\mu=\mu_s\mid\mathcal F_0\}\)пјҢ\(\sum_{s=1}^n\Pi_0(\mu_s)=1\)гҖҒ\(\Pi_0(\mu_s)\geq0\)пјӣеҗҺйӘҢпјҲ\(t\) жңҹпјү\(\Pi_t(\mu_s)=\mathbf P\{\mu=\mu_s\mid\mathcal F_t\}\)гҖӮ

16.1.2 Characterize the Belief Updating

з”ұиҙқеҸ¶ж–Ҝжі•еҲҷ (16.2)пјҡ

\(\{Z_t\}\) standard BM, \(\sigma_\delta\) known, \(\mu_\delta\) unknown, taking one fixed value from \(\{\mu_1,\dots,\mu_n\}\) that never shifts. \(\{\mathcal F_t\}\) is the filtration. The subjective prior (period 0) belief \(\Pi_0(\mu_s)=\mathbf P\{\mu=\mu_s\mid\mathcal F_0\}\), \(\sum_{s=1}^n\Pi_0(\mu_s)=1\), \(\Pi_0(\mu_s)\geq0\); the posterior (period \(t\)) \(\Pi_t(\mu_s)=\mathbf P\{\mu=\mu_s\mid\mathcal F_t\}\).

16.1.2 Characterize the Belief Updating

By Bayes' rule (16.2):

$$\Pi_{t+dt}(\mu_s)=\frac{\mathbf P\{d\delta_t\mid\mu=\mu_s;\mathcal F_t\}\cdot\Pi_t(\mu_s)}{\sum_{j=1}^n\mathbf P\{d\delta_t\mid\mu=\mu_j;\mathcal F_t\}\cdot\Pi_t(\mu_j)}.\tag{16.2}$$

з”ұ (16.1)пјҢдјјз„¶дёәжӯЈжҖҒ (16.3)пјҡ\(\mathbf P\{d\delta_t\mid\mu=\mu_s;\mathcal F_t\}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12\frac{(d\delta_t-\mu_s dt)^2}{\sigma_\delta^2 dt}}\)гҖӮд»Је…Ҙ (16.2)пјҢзәҰеҺ»е…¬еӣ еӯҗе№¶ж•ҙзҗҶеҫ— (16.4)пјҡ

By (16.1) the likelihood is normal (16.3): \(\mathbf P\{d\delta_t\mid\mu=\mu_s;\mathcal F_t\}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12\frac{(d\delta_t-\mu_s dt)^2}{\sigma_\delta^2 dt}}\). Substituting into (16.2), cancelling common factors gives (16.4):

$$\Pi_{t+dt}(\mu_s)=\frac{e^{\frac{\mu_s d\delta_t}{\sigma_\delta^2}-\frac12\frac{(\mu_s)^2}{\sigma_\delta^2}dt}\cdot\Pi_t(\mu_s)}{\sum_{j=1}^n e^{\frac{\mu_j d\delta_t}{\sigma_\delta^2}-\frac12\frac{(\mu_j)^2}{\sigma_\delta^2}dt}\cdot\Pi_t(\mu_j)}.\tag{16.4}$$

з”Ё \(e^x\approx1+x+\frac12 x^2\) дәҢйҳ¶жі°еӢ’еұ•ејҖпјҲ\(d\delta_t^2=\sigma_\delta^2 dt\)пјҢдёўејғ \(\circ(dt)\)пјүеҫ— (16.5)пјҡ\(e^{\frac{\mu_s d\delta_t}{\sigma_\delta^2}-\frac12\frac{(\mu_s)^2}{\sigma_\delta^2}dt}\approx1+\frac{\mu_s d\delta_t}{\sigma_\delta^2}\)гҖӮд»Је…Ҙ (16.4) е№¶ж•ҙзҗҶпјҲ\(\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\equiv\sum_j\mu_j\Pi_t(\mu_j)\)пјүеҫ—дҝЎеҝөеҠЁжҖҒ (16.6) дёҺеҲӣж–° (16.7)пјҡ

The second-order Taylor expansion \(e^x\approx1+x+\frac12 x^2\) (with \(d\delta_t^2=\sigma_\delta^2 dt\), dropping \(\circ(dt)\)) gives (16.5): \(e^{\frac{\mu_s d\delta_t}{\sigma_\delta^2}-\frac12\frac{(\mu_s)^2}{\sigma_\delta^2}dt}\approx1+\frac{\mu_s d\delta_t}{\sigma_\delta^2}\). Substituting into (16.4) and simplifying (with \(\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\equiv\sum_j\mu_j\Pi_t(\mu_j)\)) gives the belief dynamics (16.6) and innovation (16.7):

$$d\Pi_t(\mu_s)=\Pi_t(\mu_s)\,(\mu_s-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta])\,\frac{1}{\sigma_\delta}\,d\hat Z_t\tag{16.6}$$

$$d\hat Z_t=\frac{d\delta_t-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\,dt}{\sigma_\delta}\tag{16.7}$$

Note

Remark 16.1. (16.7) зҡ„ \(d\hat Z_t\) дёҺ (16.1) зҡ„ \(dZ_t\) дёҚеңЁеҗҢдёҖдҝЎжҒҜжөҒдёӢгҖӮжҲ‘д»¬еҒҮи®ҫ \(\sigma_\delta\) е·ІзҹҘпјӣ\(\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\) еңЁ \(t\) е·ІзҹҘпјҲ\(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) еңЁ \(t\) е·ІзҹҘпјүпјӣ\(d\delta_t\) еңЁ \(t\) еҸҜи§ӮжөӢгҖӮж•… \(d\hat Z_t\) еңЁ \(t\) еҸҜи§ӮжөӢпјҲеұһдәҺ \(\hat Z_t\) з”ҹжҲҗзҡ„ \(\hat{\mathcal F}_t\)пјүпјҢиҖҢ \(dZ_t\) жҳҜ \(t\) ж—¶зҡ„еҶІеҮ»гҖҒдёҚеңЁ \(\mathcal F_t\) дёӯгҖӮ

Remark 16.2. д№ҹеҸҜжҠҠ \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}_{s=1}^n\) зҗҶи§Јдёә \(n\) дёӘеҪұе“ҚзәўеҲ©жјӮз§»зҡ„жҪңеңЁзҠ¶жҖҒеҸҳйҮҸпјӣжӯӨиҜ йҮҠдёӢ \(d\delta_t\) жңүеҠЁжҖҒдҪҶ \(\mu_\delta\) ж— дёҚзЎ®е®ҡжҖ§вҖ”вҖ”дёӨз§ҚиҜ йҮҠдёҚзҹӣзӣҫпјҢеҸӘжҳҜеҗҢдёҖз»“жһ„зҡ„дёҚеҗҢиҜ»жі•гҖӮ

и§ӮеҜҹгҖӮ в‘  \(\mathbb E_t[d\hat Z_t]=0\)гҖҒ\(\mathrm{Var}(d\hat Z_t)=\mathrm{Var}(dZ_t)=dt\)пјҢж•… \(d\hat Z_t\sim\mathcal N(0,dt)\)пјҢ\(\{\hat Z_t\}\) д№ҹжҳҜж ҮеҮҶеёғжң—иҝҗеҠЁгҖӮв‘Ў \(\Pi_t(\mu_s)=1\) жҲ– \(0\) ж—¶ \(d\Pi_t(\mu_s)=0\)пјҡиӢҘе®Ңе…ЁпјҲжҰӮзҺҮ 1пјүзЎ®дҝЎеңЁ/дёҚеңЁжҹҗзҠ¶жҖҒпјҢдё»и§ӮжҰӮзҺҮдёҚеҶҚеҸҳгҖӮв‘ў еҸҜйӘҢиҜҒ \(\sum_s\Pi_t(\mu_s)=1\)гҖҒ\(\Pi_t(\mu_s)\geq0\) еҜ№жүҖжңү \(t\) жҲҗз«ӢпјҲдҝЎеҝөзў°еҲ° 0 еҗҺеҠЁжҖҒз«ӢеҚіеҒңжӯўпјҢдёҚдјҡеҸҳиҙҹпјӣ\(\sum_s d\Pi_t(\mu_s)=0\)пјүгҖӮ

16.1.3 Special Case: Homogeneity of Agents

иҝӣдёҖжӯҘи®ҫд»ЈзҗҶдәәеңЁйЈҺйҷ©е®№еҝҚ \(\rho_i\)гҖҒеҲқе§ӢиҙўеҜҢд»Ҫйўқ \(\omega_i\) дёҠд№ҹеҗҢиҙЁгҖӮ\(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) д»Қж»Ўи¶і (16.6)гҖҒ(16.7)гҖӮ

Note

Remark 16.1. The \(d\hat Z_t\) in (16.7) and the \(dZ_t\) in (16.1) are not in the same filtration. We assumed \(\sigma_\delta\) known; \(\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\) is known at \(t\) (since \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) is); \(d\delta_t\) is observable at \(t\). So \(d\hat Z_t\) is observable at \(t\) (in \(\hat{\mathcal F}_t\) generated by \(\hat Z_t\)), while \(dZ_t\) is the shock at \(t\), not in \(\mathcal F_t\).

Remark 16.2. One can also view \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}_{s=1}^n\) as \(n\) latent state variables affecting the dividend drift; under this reading \(d\delta_t\) has dynamics but \(\mu_\delta\) has no uncertainty вҖ” the two readings are consistent, just different views of the same structure.

Observations. в‘  \(\mathbb E_t[d\hat Z_t]=0\), \(\mathrm{Var}(d\hat Z_t)=\mathrm{Var}(dZ_t)=dt\), so \(d\hat Z_t\sim\mathcal N(0,dt)\) and \(\{\hat Z_t\}\) is also standard BM. в‘Ў When \(\Pi_t(\mu_s)=1\) or \(0\), \(d\Pi_t(\mu_s)=0\): if the agent is fully (probability one) certain of being in/out of a state, the subjective probability stops moving. в‘ў One verifies \(\sum_s\Pi_t(\mu_s)=1\), \(\Pi_t(\mu_s)\geq0\) for all \(t\) (the dynamics stop the moment a belief hits zero, so it cannot go negative; and \(\sum_s d\Pi_t(\mu_s)=0\)).

16.1.3 Special Case: Homogeneity of Agents

Further assume agents are also homogeneous in risk tolerance \(\rho_i\) and initial wealth share \(\omega_i\). \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) still satisfies (16.6), (16.7).

з”ұ (16.1)пјҢ\(\delta_{t+\tau}=\delta_t+\mu_\delta\tau+\sigma_\delta\sqrt\tau x_t\) (16.8)пјҢ\(x_t\sim\mathcal N(0,1)\)пјҲеҗҢ (15.89)пјҢ\(\mu_\delta\) д»ҚжҳҜеёёж•°пјҢеҸӘжҳҜжңӘзҹҘпјүгҖӮSDF \(\pi_t=\frac1\xi e^{-\phi t-\gamma\delta_t}\)пјҲеҗҢиҙЁ CRRAпјҢз”ұ (15.93)пјүгҖӮжңҹжңӣеҜ№ \(x_t\) дёҺ \(\mu_\delta\) еҗҢж—¶еҸ–пјҲдәҢиҖ…зҡҶжңӘзҹҘпјүгҖӮз”ұ (15.94) з®—д»·ж ј (16.9)гҖҒд»·ж ј-зәўеҲ©жҜ” (16.10)пјҡ

By (16.1), \(\delta_{t+\tau}=\delta_t+\mu_\delta\tau+\sigma_\delta\sqrt\tau x_t\) (16.8), \(x_t\sim\mathcal N(0,1)\) (same as (15.89); \(\mu_\delta\) is still a constant, just unknown). The SDF \(\pi_t=\frac1\xi e^{-\phi t-\gamma\delta_t}\) (homogeneous CRRA, from (15.93)). The expectation is taken over both \(x_t\) and \(\mu_\delta\) (both unknown). By (15.94) the price (16.9) and price-dividend ratio (16.10):

$$\frac{P_t}{D_t}=\sum_{s=1}^n\Pi_t(\mu_s)\,k(\mu_s),\qquad k(\mu_s)=\frac{1}{\phi+(\gamma-1)\mu_s-\frac12(\gamma-1)^2\sigma_\delta^2}.\tag{16.11}$$

Note

Remark 16.3. \(k(\mu_s)\) жҳҜжқЎд»¶д»·ж ј-зәўеҲ©жҜ”пјҡз”ұ (15.95)пјҢ\(k(\mu_s)=\frac{P_t}{D_t}\big|_{\mu_\delta=\mu_s}\)гҖӮ

йЈҺйҷ©жәўд»·гҖӮ з”ұ (1.53)пјҢCRRA дёӢ \(\mathbb E[r_t]-r_{f,t}=\gamma\sigma_P\sigma_C\rho_{PC}\) (16.12)пјҢзӯүд»·дәҺ (16.13)пјҡ\((\mathbb E[r_t]-r_{f,t})\,dt=\gamma(\frac{dP_t}{P_t})(\frac{dC_t}{C_t})\)гҖӮи®ҫ \(D_t=C_t\)пјҢеҲҷ \(\frac{dC_t}{C_t}=d\delta_t=\mu_\delta\,dt+\sigma_\delta\,dZ_t\)пјҢ\(\sigma_C=\sigma_\delta\)гҖҒ\(dZ_t^C=dZ_t\)гҖӮ

ж— еӯҰд№ ж—¶пјҡ \(\frac{P_t}{D_t}\) дёәеёёж•° \(h\)пјҢ\(\frac{dP_t}{P_t}=\frac{dD_t}{D_t}=d\delta_t\)пјҢ\(\sigma_P=\sigma_\delta\)пјҢд»Је…Ҙ (16.13) еҫ— (16.14)пјҡ

Note

Remark 16.3. \(k(\mu_s)\) is the conditional price-dividend ratio: by (15.95), \(k(\mu_s)=\frac{P_t}{D_t}\big|_{\mu_\delta=\mu_s}\).

Risk premium. By (1.53), under CRRA \(\mathbb E[r_t]-r_{f,t}=\gamma\sigma_P\sigma_C\rho_{PC}\) (16.12), equivalently (16.13): \((\mathbb E[r_t]-r_{f,t})\,dt=\gamma(\frac{dP_t}{P_t})(\frac{dC_t}{C_t})\). With \(D_t=C_t\), \(\frac{dC_t}{C_t}=d\delta_t=\mu_\delta\,dt+\sigma_\delta\,dZ_t\), \(\sigma_C=\sigma_\delta\), \(dZ_t^C=dZ_t\).

Without learning: \(\frac{P_t}{D_t}\) is a constant \(h\), \(\frac{dP_t}{P_t}=\frac{dD_t}{D_t}=d\delta_t\), \(\sigma_P=\sigma_\delta\); substituting into (16.13) gives (16.14):

$$\mathbb E[r_t]-r_{f,t}=\gamma\sigma_\delta^2.\tag{16.14}$$

жңүеӯҰд№ ж—¶пјҡ \(\sigma_C=\sigma_\delta\)гҖҒ\(dZ_t^C=dZ_t\) дёҚеҸҳпјҢдҪҶз”ұ (16.10) \(\frac{P_t}{D_t}\) дёҚеҶҚеёёж•°пјҢйңҖеҜ№ (16.9) еҫ®еҲҶгҖӮз»“жһң (16.15)пјҡ\(\frac{dP_t}{P_t}=[\sigma_\delta+(\tilde{\mathbb E}[\mu_s]-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta])\frac1{\sigma_\delta}]\,dZ_t+\circ(dt)\)пјҢе…¶дёӯ \(\tilde{\mathbb E}[\mu_s]\) жҳҜеңЁеҠ жқғжөӢеәҰ \(\tilde\Pi_t(\mu_s)=\frac{\Pi_t(\mu_s)k(\mu_s)}{\sum_s\Pi_t(\mu_s)k(\mu_s)}\) дёӢзҡ„жңҹжңӣгҖӮд»Је…Ҙ (16.13) еҫ—жңүеӯҰд№ йЈҺйҷ©жәўд»· (16.16)пјҡ

With learning: \(\sigma_C=\sigma_\delta\), \(dZ_t^C=dZ_t\) unchanged, but by (16.10) \(\frac{P_t}{D_t}\) is no longer constant, so we differentiate (16.9). The result (16.15): \(\frac{dP_t}{P_t}=[\sigma_\delta+(\tilde{\mathbb E}[\mu_s]-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta])\frac1{\sigma_\delta}]\,dZ_t+\circ(dt)\), where \(\tilde{\mathbb E}[\mu_s]\) is the expectation under the weighted measure \(\tilde\Pi_t(\mu_s)=\frac{\Pi_t(\mu_s)k(\mu_s)}{\sum_s\Pi_t(\mu_s)k(\mu_s)}\). Substituting into (16.13) gives the with-learning risk premium (16.16):

$$\mathbb E[r_t]-r_{f,t}=\gamma\sigma_\delta\left[\sigma_\delta+\left(\tilde{\mathbb E}[\mu_s]-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\right)\frac{1}{\sigma_\delta}\right].\tag{16.16}$$

жҜ”иҫғгҖӮ жңүеӯҰд№ йЈҺйҷ©жәўд»· $<$ ж— еӯҰд№ йЈҺйҷ©жәўд»· еҪ“дё”д»…еҪ“ \(\tilde{\mathbb E}[\mu_s]-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]<0\)пјҢеҚіеҠ жқғжөӢеәҰ \(\tilde\Pi\) жҜ” \(\Pi\) жӣҙеҒҸеҗ‘е°Ҹ \(\mu_s\)пјҢиҝҷеҸ‘з”ҹеңЁ \(k'(\mu_s)<0\)пјҲеҚі \(\gamma>1\)пјҢIES \(\frac1\gamma<1\)пјүж—¶гҖӮзӣҙи§үпјҡIES $<1$ ж—¶ж¶Ҳиҙ№еҜ№д»·ж јдёҚеӨӘж•Ҹж„ҹпјҢж•… \(\rho_{PC}\) еҮҸе°ҸгҖҒйЈҺйҷ©жәўд»·иў«еӯҰд№ еҺӢдҪҺгҖӮ

16.1.4 Heterogeneity of Agents

жҒўеӨҚејӮиҙЁйЈҺйҷ©е®№еҝҚ \(\rho_i\) дёҺеҲқе§Ӣд»Ҫйўқ \(\omega_i\)пјҢдҪҶдҝқжҢҒдҝЎеҝөеҗҢиҙЁгҖӮ\(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) д»Қж»Ўи¶і (16.6)гҖҒ(16.7)гҖӮз”ұ (15.92)пјҢз»ҷе®ҡ \(\mu_\delta\) ж—¶ (16.17)пјҡ\(P_t\mid\mu_\delta=e^{g(\delta_t)}\frac{1-e^{-\phi(T-t)}}\phi\mathbb E_t^{x_t,\tau}[e^{\delta_{t+\tau}-g(\delta_{t+\tau})}\mid\mu_\delta]\)гҖӮеҜ№жңӘзҹҘ \(\mu_\delta\) з”ЁдҝЎеҝөеҠ жқғеҫ—д»·ж ј (16.18)гҖҒ(16.19)пјҡ

Comparison. The with-learning premium $<$ without-learning premium iff \(\tilde{\mathbb E}[\mu_s]-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]<0\), i.e. the weighted measure \(\tilde\Pi\) tilts toward smaller \(\mu_s\) than \(\Pi\), which happens when \(k'(\mu_s)<0\) (i.e. \(\gamma>1\), IES \(\frac1\gamma<1\)). Intuition: with IES $<1$ consumption is less responsive to prices, so \(\rho_{PC}\) shrinks and learning lowers the risk premium.

16.1.4 Heterogeneity of Agents

Restore heterogeneous risk tolerance \(\rho_i\) and initial share \(\omega_i\), but keep beliefs homogeneous. \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) still satisfies (16.6), (16.7). By (15.92), given \(\mu_\delta\) (16.17): \(P_t\mid\mu_\delta=e^{g(\delta_t)}\frac{1-e^{-\phi(T-t)}}\phi\mathbb E_t^{x_t,\tau}[e^{\delta_{t+\tau}-g(\delta_{t+\tau})}\mid\mu_\delta]\). Belief-weighting over the unknown \(\mu_\delta\) gives the price (16.18), (16.19):

$$P_t=D_t\sum_{s=1}^n\Pi_t(\mu_s)\,k(\delta_t,\mu_s),\qquad\frac{P_t}{D_t}=\sum_{s=1}^n\Pi_t(\mu_s)\,k(\delta_t,\mu_s).\tag{16.19}$$

е…¶дёӯ \(k(\delta_t,\mu_s)\equiv e^{g(\delta_t)-\delta_t}\frac{1-e^{-\phi(T-t)}}\phi\mathbb E_t^{x_t,\tau}[e^{\delta_{t+\tau}-g(\delta_{t+\tau})}\mid\mu_s]\) еҗҢж—¶дҫқиө– \(\delta_t\)пјҲеҸҜи§ӮжөӢпјүдёҺ \(\mu_s\)пјҲдёҚеҸҜи§ӮжөӢпјүгҖӮ\(g(\delta_t)\) з”ұжіӣеҮҪдёҚеҠЁзӮ№ (15.76) й’үдҪҸдҪҶйҡҫи§ЈпјҢе…¶еҜјж•°еҸҜеҰӮ В§15.3.3 еҲ»з”»пјҲ\(g'(\delta_t)=1/\mathbb E_{\mathrm{CS}}^\star[\rho_i\mid\delta_t]>0\)гҖҒ\(g''(\delta_t)<0\)пјүгҖӮеҸҜж•°еҖјжЁЎжӢҹ (16.20)пјҡе…ҲжҢү \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) жҠҪ \(\mu_\delta\)гҖҒжҢүжҲӘж–ӯжҢҮж•°еҲҶеёғжҠҪ \(\tau\)гҖҒжҢүжӯЈжҖҒжҠҪ \(\delta_{t+\tau}\)пјҢи’ҷзү№еҚЎжҙӣе№іеқҮдј°и®Ў \(\mathbb E_t^{x_t,\tau,\mu_\delta}[e^{\delta_{t+\tau}-g(\delta_{t+\tau})}]\)пјҢеҶҚз®— \(P_t\)гҖӮ

16.1.5 Regime Shifting Model

ж”ҫжқҫ"\(\mu_\delta\) дёәеёёж•°"пјҡи®ҫ \(\mu_{\delta,t}\) еҸҜеңЁ \(\{\mu_1,\dots,\mu_n\}\) й—ҙжқҘеӣһеҲҮжҚўпјҲзҠ¶жҖҒеҲҮжҚўпјүгҖӮжӯӨж—¶ \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) дёҚеҶҚж»Ўи¶і (16.6)гҖҒ(16.7)гҖӮи®ҫд»ЈзҗҶдәәеҜ№еҗҢдёҖзҰ»ж•ЈзҠ¶жҖҒйӣҶжңүеҗҢиҙЁдҝЎеҝөпјҢжІҝз”Ё В§16.1.1 еҒҮи®ҫдҪҶе…Ғи®ё \(\mu_{\delta,t}\) ж—¶еҸҳгҖӮе®ҡд№үиҪ¬з§»зҺҮ \(\Lambda=\{\lambda_{ij}\}\)пјҡ\(\lambda_{ij}=\mathbf P\{\mu_{\delta,t+dt}=\mu_j\mid\mu_{\delta,t}=\mu_i\}\)пјҢ\(\sum_j\lambda_{ij}=1\)гҖӮдҝЎеҝөеҠЁжҖҒеҲҶдёӨйғЁеҲҶпјҡ

where \(k(\delta_t,\mu_s)\equiv e^{g(\delta_t)-\delta_t}\frac{1-e^{-\phi(T-t)}}\phi\mathbb E_t^{x_t,\tau}[e^{\delta_{t+\tau}-g(\delta_{t+\tau})}\mid\mu_s]\) depends on both \(\delta_t\) (observable) and \(\mu_s\) (unobservable). \(g(\delta_t)\) is pinned by the functional fixed point (15.76) but hard to solve; its derivatives can be characterized as in В§15.3.3 (\(g'(\delta_t)=1/\mathbb E_{\mathrm{CS}}^\star[\rho_i\mid\delta_t]>0\), \(g''(\delta_t)<0\)). Numerically simulable (16.20): draw \(\mu_\delta\) from \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\), \(\tau\) from the truncated exponential, \(\delta_{t+\tau}\) from the normal, Monte-Carlo average to estimate \(\mathbb E_t^{x_t,\tau,\mu_\delta}[e^{\delta_{t+\tau}-g(\delta_{t+\tau})}]\), then compute \(P_t\).

16.1.5 Regime Shifting Model

Relax "\(\mu_\delta\) constant": let \(\mu_{\delta,t}\) shift back and forth within \(\{\mu_1,\dots,\mu_n\}\) (regime shifting). Now \(\{\Pi_t(\mu_s)\}\) no longer satisfies (16.6), (16.7). Agents have homogeneous beliefs over the same discrete state set; keep В§16.1.1 assumptions but allow \(\mu_{\delta,t}\) to vary in time. Define transition rates \(\Lambda=\{\lambda_{ij}\}\): \(\lambda_{ij}=\mathbf P\{\mu_{\delta,t+dt}=\mu_j\mid\mu_{\delta,t}=\mu_i\}\), \(\sum_j\lambda_{ij}=1\). The belief dynamics split into two parts:

$$d\Pi_t(\mu_i)=\underbrace{\left[\Pi_t(\mu_i)(\mu_i-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta])\frac{1}{\sigma_\delta}d\hat Z_t\right]\left(\sum_{i=1}^n\lambda_{ii}\right)}_{\text{Part A: regime unchanged}}+\underbrace{\sum_{j\neq i}\lambda_{ji}\Pi_t(\mu_j)-\sum_{j\neq i}\lambda_{ij}\Pi_t(\mu_i)}_{\text{Part B: regime changed}},$$

е…¶дёӯ \(d\hat Z_t=\frac{d\delta_t-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\,dt}{\sigma_\delta}\)гҖӮPart A жҳҜ"еҢәеҲ¶дёҚеҸҳ"жқЎд»¶дёӢзҡ„иҙқеҸ¶ж–ҜеӯҰд№ пјҲеҗҢ (16.6)пјүпјҢPart B жҳҜи·ЁеҢәеҲ¶еҲҮжҚўеёҰжқҘзҡ„жҰӮзҺҮжөҒе…ҘжөҒеҮәгҖӮ

Note

Remark 16.4. иҝҷйҮҢеҒҮи®ҫиҪ¬з§»зҺҮзҹ©йҳө \(\Lambda\) е·ІзҹҘпјӣеҗҰеҲҷйңҖеҶҚеҠ дёҖеұӮеҜ№ \(\Lambda\) зҡ„еӯҰд№ пјҢй—®йўҳжӣҙеӨҚжқӮгҖӮ

with \(d\hat Z_t=\frac{d\delta_t-\mathbb E_t[\mu_\delta]\,dt}{\sigma_\delta}\). Part A is Bayesian learning conditional on the regime not changing (same as (16.6)); Part B is the probability inflow/outflow from regime switches.

Note

Remark 16.4. Here \(\Lambda\) is assumed known; otherwise the problem is complicated by another layer of learning on \(\Lambda\).

16.2.1 Setup

Observation equation (16.21):

$$d\mathbf Y_t=(\mathbf F_0(\mathbf Y_t)+\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)\beta_t)\,dt+\boldsymbol\omega\,d\mathbf Z_t\tag{16.21}$$

е…¶дёӯ \(\mathbf Y_t,\mathbf F_0,\mathbf F_1,d\mathbf Z_t\) еқҮ \(n\times1\)пјҢ\(\boldsymbol\omega\) дёә \(n\times n\)пјҢ\(\beta_t\) дёә \(1\times1\)пјҢ\(d\mathbf Z_t\sim\mathcal N(\mathbf 0,\mathbf I\,dt)\)гҖӮзҠ¶жҖҒж–№зЁӢ (16.22)пјҡ

with \(\mathbf Y_t,\mathbf F_0,\mathbf F_1,d\mathbf Z_t\) all \(n\times1\), \(\boldsymbol\omega\) being \(n\times n\), \(\beta_t\) being \(1\times1\), \(d\mathbf Z_t\sim\mathcal N(\mathbf 0,\mathbf I\,dt)\). State equation (16.22):

$$d\beta_t=(\alpha_0+\alpha_1\beta_t)\,dt+\boldsymbol\eta'\,d\mathbf Z_t\tag{16.22}$$

\(\boldsymbol\eta\) дёә \(n\times1\)пјҢ\(\alpha_0,\alpha_1\) дёә \(1\times1\)пјҢ(16.21)гҖҒ(16.22) дёӯ \(d\mathbf Z_t\) зӣёеҗҢгҖӮеҒҮи®ҫпјҡи§ӮжөӢеҗ‘йҮҸ \(\mathbf Y_t\) жҜҸжңҹеҸҜи§ӮжөӢпјҢзҠ¶жҖҒ \(\beta_t\) дёҚеҸҜи§ӮжөӢпјӣ\(\beta_t\) зҡ„е…ҲйӘҢпјҲ\(t\) ж—¶гҖҒ\(d\mathbf Y_t\) е®һзҺ°еүҚпјүеҲҶеёғ \(\beta_t\sim\mathcal N(\hat\beta_t,v_t)\)гҖӮ

16.2.2 Multivariate Normal Distribution

з”ұеӨҡе…ғжӯЈжҖҒе®ҡд№үпјҲHe 2019a В§21пјүпјҢ\(\mathbf Y_{t+dt}\) дёҺ \(\beta_{t+dt}\) еңЁ \(t\) ж—¶пјҲ\(d\mathbf Z_t\) е®һзҺ°еүҚпјүжңҚд»ҺиҒ”еҗҲжӯЈжҖҒ (16.23)пјҡ

\(\boldsymbol\eta\) is \(n\times1\), \(\alpha_0,\alpha_1\) are \(1\times1\), and \(d\mathbf Z_t\) in (16.21), (16.22) is the same. Assumptions: the observation vector \(\mathbf Y_t\) is observed each period, the state \(\beta_t\) is not; the prior (at \(t\), before \(d\mathbf Y_t\)) distribution \(\beta_t\sim\mathcal N(\hat\beta_t,v_t)\).

16.2.2 Multivariate Normal Distribution

By the multivariate-normal definition (He 2019a В§21), \(\mathbf Y_{t+dt}\) and \(\beta_{t+dt}\) are jointly normal at \(t\) (before \(d\mathbf Z_t\)) (16.23):

$$\begin{pmatrix}\mathbf Y_{t+dt}\\\beta_{t+dt}\end{pmatrix}\sim\mathcal N\!\left(\begin{pmatrix}\hat{\mathbf Y}_{(t+dt)\mid t}\\\hat\beta_{(t+dt)\mid t}\end{pmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'\mid t}&\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\beta\mid t}\\\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\beta\mathbf Y'\mid t}&\Sigma_{\beta\beta\mid t}\end{bmatrix}\right).\tag{16.23}$$

еҗ„йЎ№з”ұ (16.21)гҖҒ(16.22) еҸ–жқЎд»¶жңҹжңӣ/ж–№е·®з®—еҮәпјҡеқҮеҖј (16.24)гҖҒ(16.25)пјҡ\(\hat{\mathbf Y}_{(t+dt)\mid t}=\mathbf Y_t+(\mathbf F_0(\mathbf Y_t)+\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)\hat\beta_t)\,dt\)гҖҒ\(\hat\beta_{(t+dt)\mid t}=\hat\beta_t+(\alpha_0+\alpha_1\hat\beta_t)\,dt\)пјӣеҚҸж–№е·® (16.28)гҖҒ(16.31)гҖҒ(16.32)гҖҒ(16.33)пјҡ

Each term follows from conditional expectations/variances of (16.21), (16.22): means (16.24), (16.25): \(\hat{\mathbf Y}_{(t+dt)\mid t}=\mathbf Y_t+(\mathbf F_0(\mathbf Y_t)+\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)\hat\beta_t)\,dt\), \(\hat\beta_{(t+dt)\mid t}=\hat\beta_t+(\alpha_0+\alpha_1\hat\beta_t)\,dt\); covariances (16.28), (16.31), (16.32), (16.33):

$$\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'\mid t}=\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega'\,dt,\quad\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\beta\mid t}=(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)v_t+\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\eta)\,dt,\quad\Sigma_{\beta\beta\mid t}=v_t+(2\alpha_1 v_t+\boldsymbol\eta'\boldsymbol\eta)\,dt.\tag{16.33}$$

пјҲжҺЁеҜјдёӯеҸҚеӨҚз”Ё \((dt)^2=0\)гҖҒ\(d\mathbf Z_t\,dt=\mathbf 0\)гҖҒ\(d\mathbf Z_t(d\mathbf Z_t)'=\mathbf I_{n\times n}\,dt\)гҖӮпјү

16.2.3 Updating

Lemma 16.1пјҲжқЎд»¶жӯЈжҖҒпјү. иӢҘ \(\begin{pmatrix}\mathbf X\\\mathbf Y\end{pmatrix}\sim\mathcal N\!\left(\begin{pmatrix}\mathbf 0\\\mathbf 0\end{pmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'}&\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf Y'}\\\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf X'}&\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'}\end{bmatrix}\right)\)пјҢеҲҷ \(\mathbf X\mid\mathbf Y\sim\mathcal N(\mathbf A\mathbf Y,\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'\mid\mathbf Y})\)пјҢе…¶дёӯ \(\mathbf A=\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf Y'}\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'}^{-1}\)гҖҒ\(\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'\mid\mathbf Y}=\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'}-\mathbf A\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'}\mathbf A'\)гҖӮ

жҠҠ (16.23) ж”№еҶҷдёәеҺ»дёӯеҝғеҢ–еҪўејҸ (16.34)пјҢеҜ№ \((\mathbf Y_{t+dt}-\hat{\mathbf Y}_{(t+dt)\mid t},\ \beta_{t+dt}-\hat\beta_{(t+dt)\mid t})\) еә”з”Ё Lemma 16.1пјҢеҫ—жӣҙж–°еҗҺ \(\beta_{t+dt}\mid\mathbf Y_{t+dt}\sim\mathcal N(\hat\beta_{t+dt},v_{t+dt})\)пјҢе…¶дёӯ \(\mathbf A=\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\beta\mathbf Y'\mid t}\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'\mid t}^{-1}\) (16.35)гҖҒ\(v_{t+dt}=\Sigma_{\beta\beta\mid t}-\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\beta\mathbf Y'\mid t}\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'\mid t}^{-1}\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\beta\mid t}\) (16.36)гҖӮд»Је…Ҙ (16.28)гҖҒ(16.32) еҫ— \(\mathbf A=(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)v_t+\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\eta)'(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega')^{-1}\)пјҢиҝӣиҖҢйҡҗеҗ«зҠ¶жҖҒзҡ„еқҮеҖјеҠЁжҖҒ (16.37)пјҡ

(The derivation repeatedly uses \((dt)^2=0\), \(d\mathbf Z_t\,dt=\mathbf 0\), \(d\mathbf Z_t(d\mathbf Z_t)'=\mathbf I_{n\times n}\,dt\).)

16.2.3 Updating

Lemma 16.1 (conditional normal). If \(\begin{pmatrix}\mathbf X\\\mathbf Y\end{pmatrix}\sim\mathcal N\!\left(\begin{pmatrix}\mathbf 0\\\mathbf 0\end{pmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'}&\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf Y'}\\\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf X'}&\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'}\end{bmatrix}\right)\), then \(\mathbf X\mid\mathbf Y\sim\mathcal N(\mathbf A\mathbf Y,\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'\mid\mathbf Y})\) with \(\mathbf A=\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf Y'}\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'}^{-1}\), \(\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'\mid\mathbf Y}=\mathbf S_{\mathbf X\mathbf X'}-\mathbf A\mathbf S_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'}\mathbf A'\).

Rewriting (16.23) in demeaned form (16.34) and applying Lemma 16.1 to \((\mathbf Y_{t+dt}-\hat{\mathbf Y}_{(t+dt)\mid t},\ \beta_{t+dt}-\hat\beta_{(t+dt)\mid t})\) gives the updated \(\beta_{t+dt}\mid\mathbf Y_{t+dt}\sim\mathcal N(\hat\beta_{t+dt},v_{t+dt})\), with \(\mathbf A=\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\beta\mathbf Y'\mid t}\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'\mid t}^{-1}\) (16.35), \(v_{t+dt}=\Sigma_{\beta\beta\mid t}-\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\beta\mathbf Y'\mid t}\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\mathbf Y'\mid t}^{-1}\boldsymbol\Sigma_{\mathbf Y\beta\mid t}\) (16.36). Substituting (16.28), (16.32) gives \(\mathbf A=(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)v_t+\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\eta)'(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega')^{-1}\), hence the latent state's mean dynamics (16.37):

$$d\hat\beta_t=(\alpha_0+\alpha_1\hat\beta_t)\,dt+(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)v_t+\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\eta)'(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega')^{-1}\,d\tilde{\mathbf Z}_t,\tag{16.37}$$

е…¶дёӯ \(d\tilde{\mathbf Z}_t=(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega')^{-\frac12}[d\mathbf Y_t-(\mathbf F_0(\mathbf Y_t)+\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)\hat\beta_t)\,dt]\) ж»Ўи¶і \(d\tilde{\mathbf Z}_t\sim\mathcal N(\mathbf 0,\mathbf I\,dt)\)пјҲеӣ  (16.21) з»ҷеҮә \(d\mathbf Y_t\) зҡ„еқҮеҖјдёҺж–№е·® \(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega'\)пјүгҖӮд»Је…Ҙ (16.33)гҖҒ(16.31) еҫ—ж–№е·®еҠЁжҖҒ (16.39)пјҡ

where \(d\tilde{\mathbf Z}_t=(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega')^{-\frac12}[d\mathbf Y_t-(\mathbf F_0(\mathbf Y_t)+\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)\hat\beta_t)\,dt]\) satisfies \(d\tilde{\mathbf Z}_t\sim\mathcal N(\mathbf 0,\mathbf I\,dt)\) (since (16.21) gives the mean and variance \(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega'\) of \(d\mathbf Y_t\)). Substituting (16.33), (16.31) gives the variance dynamics (16.39):

$$\frac{dv_t}{dt}=(2\alpha_1 v_t+\boldsymbol\eta'\boldsymbol\eta)-(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)v_t+\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\eta)'(\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\omega')^{-1}(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)v_t+\boldsymbol\omega\boldsymbol\eta).\tag{16.39}$$

  • (16.39) жҳҜ \(v_t\) зҡ„еұҖйғЁзЎ®е®ҡжҖ§иҝҗеҠЁпјҲ\(t\) ж—¶ \(\frac{dv_t}{dt}\) ж— йҡҸжңәжҖ§пјүгҖӮ
  • иӢҘ \(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)=\mathbf F_1\) жҒ’дёәеёёж•°пјҢ(16.39) иЎЁжҳҺ \(v_t\) ж”¶ж•ӣеҲ°дҪҝ \(\frac{dv_t}{dt}=0\) зҡ„е®ҡеҖјпјҲеҸҜз”ұ (16.39) жҳҫејҸи§ЈеҮәпјҢRiccati дёҚеҠЁзӮ№пјүгҖӮ
  • иӢҘ \(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)\) дҫқиө– \(\mathbf Y_t\)пјҢеҲҷдёҖиҲ¬дёҚж”¶ж•ӣгҖӮ
  • (16.39) is a locally deterministic motion of \(v_t\) (\(\frac{dv_t}{dt}\) has no randomness at \(t\)).
  • If \(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)=\mathbf F_1\) is always constant, (16.39) shows \(v_t\) converges to the value where \(\frac{dv_t}{dt}=0\) (explicitly solvable from (16.39), a Riccati fixed point).
  • If \(\mathbf F_1(\mathbf Y_t)\) depends on \(\mathbf Y_t\), convergence generally fails.
